Like many first-years, I was naive with high hopes for finding my first co-op/internship. However, like many first years, I was hit with reality.

There is really nothing much to say apart from your first co-op/internship search is going to be tough. As a newbie, I mainly searched on WaterlooWorks, barely looking at LinkedIn.

With zero professional experience and a lot of side projects, I quickly realized the value of experience compared to projects.

I applied to over 400 jobs, got only 1 interview and didn't move forward. In the end, my dad knew someone who was looking for interns, and so I spent my first co-op working at Twimbit as an Advisory Maverick.

Looking back, I kind of wish I had done the internship outside WaterlooWorks. This ties onto the next point that, although my first search was devastating, I wasn't fazed by it much.

Maybe because I had a 'rejection' mindset from the get go.
